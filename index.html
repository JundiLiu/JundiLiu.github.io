Biography

I am a PhD student in Industrial & Systems Engineering Department at University of Washington. I am co-advised by Prof. Linda Boyle who leads Human Factors and Statistical Modeling (HFSM) Lab and Prof. Ashis Banerjee who leads Scale-independent Multimodal Automated Real Time Systems (SMARTS) Lab. Prior to studying at UW, I received my bachelor degree in Computer Science and Engineering from Shanghai Jiao Tong University.

Currently I’m working on Synergy: Augmented reality for control of reservation-based intersections with mixed autonomous-non autonomous flows funded by National Science Foundation (NSF) Cyber-Physical Systems (CPS). This work is collaborated with Prof. Peter Stone, Prof. Christian G. Claudel, and Prof. Stephen D. Boyles from the University of Texas at Austin. I used to work on Enabling Real-Time Supply Chain Visibility Through Predictive Analytics funded by The Digital Manufacturing and Design Innovation Institute (DMDII).

I’m teaching INDE 315: Probability and Statistics for Engineers at University of Washington in Summer, 2019.

Projects

Augmented reality for control of reservation-based intersections with mixed autonomous-non autonomous flows

Since it will be many decades before all vehicles are fully automated, the objective of this project is to improve the performance of reservation-based intersections with mixed human driven and autonomous vehicles, using augmented reality (AR) technology, which is already well developed and will become mainstream much sooner.

Enabling Real-Time Supply Chain Visibility Through Predictive Analytics

The scope of the project is defined by a combination of business problem (case) and corresponding technical challenges. The primary technical reason for this problem is that information flows across supply chains are limited, making it difficult to have satisfactory predictive capabilities on critical decision making considerations such as on-time parts deliveries and production demand forecasts. Consequently, the specific objective of this project is to leverage the power of supervised machine learning in utilizing historical transactional data to provide real-time information into the predicted flow of parts or materials across supply chain systems for both OEMs and SMEs.
